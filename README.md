beautiful-dead-ends
An exploration of unsolved problems in mathematics and theoretical computer science - not an attempt to solve them.
The goal here is to follow the process of mathematical reasoning as far as it will go, document what's interesting along the way, and be honest when the reasoning hits a wall. Understanding why a problem is hard is itself a form of progress. The dead ends are the point.
Each problem gets a series of Jupyter notebooks written as a research journal - first person, thinking out loud, building from first principles toward the frontier of what's known. The notebooks are meant to be read, not just run.

Problems
🔴 Collatz Conjecture

Does every positive integer eventually reach 1 under repeated application of the 3n+1 rule?

Status: in progress

🔴 Hadwiger Conjecture

Does every graph that can't be k-coloured contain a complete graph Kₖ as a minor?

Status: planned

Background

This project grew out of a conviction that mathematical reasoning is worth documenting especially when it doesn't reach a destination. The notebooks draw on undergraduate mathematics and are written to be followable by anyone with a similar background.
